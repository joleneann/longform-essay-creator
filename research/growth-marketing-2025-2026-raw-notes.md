## 1. What Growth Marketers Actually Do Day-to-Day

### Core Responsibilities

A growth marketer is responsible for first understanding the economics of the business, the target person, and the pain or value the product solves. Then they identify the best areas to run experiments and launch new marketing programs to add volume to the funnel and make it more efficient.

Day-to-day activities include:

- **Sitting in on engineering demos** to learn about the product and create more targeted messaging
- **Joining discovery calls** with prospective customers to uncover pain points and understand underlying trends, which helps develop campaigns that speak directly to customer needs
- **Solving paid ad funnels**: launching Google ads, YouTube ads, Reddit ads, Meta ads. Starts with keyword/audience targeting based on ICP hypothesis, then building creative and messaging, setting up campaigns and bid strategy, iterating daily
- **Doing things that don't scale**: talking to partners and media companies for guest posts, sending gifts via CEO's LinkedIn to set up sales meetings, building reputation in key Reddit and Slack groups, incentivizing customers to refer connections
- **Growing organic channels**: finding ways to efficiently create quality content that drives SEO growth and social engagement
- **Running experiments**: A/B tests, landing page variants, onboarding flow optimizations, pricing page tests
- **Analyzing data**: funnel analysis, cohort analysis, attribution modeling, channel performance reporting

Key insight: Increasing any step of the funnel by 10% while keeping the rest constant results in a 10% lift for the business.

### Job Descriptions at Top Companies

**Notion:**
- Affiliate Partnerships Growth Manager (B2B Growth team): drives acquisition of high-value PLG users through strategic affiliates and partnerships. Requires technical proficiency with SQL, Amplitude, Hex.
- Performance Marketing Lead: builds and owns a portfolio of performance marketing channels globally (Paid Marketing, Affiliate Marketing, Attribution/Measurement strategy).
- Head of Growth: leads digital, web and lifecycle teams plus exploratory channels (affiliates, referrals), optimizing significant marketing budget against company goals.

**Figma:**
- Growth Marketing Manager, Lifecycle: focused on lifecycle marketing within the growth org.

**Ramp:**
- Treats product advantage as primary growth channel. Runs distribution with operational seriousness of sales. Brand, creators, outbound, and product are one integrated system.
- Reached $700M annualized revenue by January 2025, then $1B+ by August-October 2025 by stacking multiple motions.

### Team Structure & Org Charts

**Three main marketing sub-functions (Emily Kramer/MKT1 model):**
1. **Brand & Content**: creates fuel (words, design, video) aligned with audience and engine
2. **Product Marketing**: research to understand audience, market, product, positioning; helps with activities requiring deep product/audience knowledge
3. **Growth Marketing**: drives full-funnel growth and revenue; distributes fuel across multiple channels

**Growth team models:**
- **Independent model**: reports directly to head of growth, runs own roadmap, pulls talent from marketing, product, engineering, data. Best for small to mid-stage startups where speed is king.
- **Matrix model**: at scale, growth marketing uses channel owners, campaign owners, creators, and ops/analytics roles.
- Growth-stage companies ($20-$200M revenue) often use a combo of in-house roles and freelancers.

**Critical success factor**: the growth team structure is best suited for startups that have achieved product-market fit and are ready to invest in aggressive growth.

## 2. Core Channels and Tactics (2025-2026 Reality)

### A. Paid Acquisition

**Google Ads:**
- Demand Gen campaigns: 26% increase in conversions per dollar driven by 60+ AI-powered improvements
- 62% of Google Ads professionals now use Smart Bidding (growing)
- Key strategies: Target CPA for lead gen, Target ROAS for e-commerce
- Smart Bidding Exploration: Google's AI temporarily lowers ROAS targets to explore new traffic opportunities
- **AI Max for Search** (late 2025 rollout, all North America by January 2026): keyword-free Search. Just provide landing page, daily budget, ROAS/CPA goals. Everything else (match types, negatives, ad copy, sitelinks) handled by Gemini 3.

**Meta:**
- 11 new AI advertising tools introduced at Cannes Lions 2025
- $14-15 billion investment in Scale AI
- By end of 2026: AI will create ads with significantly less effort
- **Lattice**: fully automated targeting across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, WhatsApp
- Full AI ad generation + automated targeting planned for 2026

**Key strategic shift**: competitive advantage is now about who can build a system that helps AI learn faster, not who manages bids better manually.

**Benchmark data:**
- B2B SaaS average CAC via paid search: $802 (2025)
- Referrals remain most cost-efficient: $141-$200 per customer
- Organic channels: $500-$1,500 per customer but better long-term returns
- CAC rising 40-60% since 2023

### B. SEO/Content and the AI Impact

**Zero-click search reality:**
- 58.5% of US Google searches and 59.7% of European searches end without a click
- AI Overviews reduce organic CTR by 61% (from 1.76% to 0.61%)
- 93% of AI Mode searches end without a click (vs 43% for AI Overviews)
- 68% of Google searches generate no clicks (2026 data)

**But citation matters:**
- Being cited in AI Overviews increases organic CTR by 35% compared to not being cited
- AI-referred visitors convert 23x higher than organic search visitors (lower volume, higher quality)
- Citation strategy (not just ranking position) is the primary metric for organic search in 2026

**AI-generated content performance:**
- Only 3% of AI-generated pages remained in top 100 after 3 months (down from 28% in month 1)
- Without authority, uniqueness, or E-E-A-T signals, rankings drop sharply
- AI referral traffic: up 357% YoY as of June 2025, but still only 1.08% of all website traffic vs 25% for traditional organic

**Answer Engine Optimization (AEO):**
- Structuring content so AI platforms can extract and cite it
- Content depth (sentence/word counts) and readability matter most for AI citations
- 44.2% of LLM citations come from first 30% of text (the intro), 31.1% from middle, 24.7% from conclusion
- Traditional SEO metrics like traffic and backlinks have little impact on AI citation likelihood

**Product-Led SEO (Eli Schwartz's framework):**
- Prioritizes user experience over traditional keyword optimization
- Conventional SEO tactics becoming obsolete; product-led strategies shape the future
- Example: SurveyMonkey grew organic search from 1% of revenue to a key driver of global revenue using this approach

### C. Product-Led Growth (PLG) Mechanics

**2025 adoption**: 91% of B2B SaaS companies are increasing PLG investment.

**Key strategies:**
- **Freemium**: suits large TAM with viral potential. Average conversion rate: 5% of freemium signups convert to paid (OpenView benchmark).
- **Free trials**: work with high ACV and complex products
- **Reverse trials**: give full product access upfront, then gate after time limit. Creates stronger activation.
- **Viral loops**: built-in sharing features (invites, collaboration tools) spread naturally. Dropbox's referral program (free storage for both referrer and new user) is the canonical example.
- **Referral programs**: CAC of $141-$200, the most cost-efficient acquisition channel

**PLG metrics:**
- PQLs achieve 25-30% conversion rates vs 5-10% for traditional MQLs
- PLG companies acquire customers at 1/10th the cost of sales-led ($100-$500 CAC vs $5K-$50K)
- PLG companies achieve 2-3x better unit economics than sales-led competitors
- Target NRR: 120%+
- Activation rate, time-to-value, and user retention rate are the most important experience KPIs

**2025 sophistication**: successful PLG requires strategies around PQL definition, Aha Moment identification, Time-to-Value optimization, and strategic feature gating.

### E. Email/Lifecycle Marketing and AI Personalization

**Key lifecycle flows:**

**Trends:**

### F. Account-Based Marketing (ABM) for B2B

**Performance benchmarks:**
- ABM creates 16% more opportunities on average, tracking to closed-won
- Companies aligning ABM with Account-Based Advertising see 60% higher win rates

**Shift**: strongest list intelligence, deeper personalization via intent data, seamless alignment between demand gen, sales, and ABM workflows.

## 3. Key Metrics Growth Marketers Own

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- B2B SaaS average: $702
- B2B paid search average: $802 (2025)
- Fintech highest: $1,450
- Referrals most efficient: $141-$200
- **CAC rising 40-60% since 2023**

### LTV:CAC Ratio
- Healthy SaaS: 3:1 minimum
- Top-tier: 4:1 or 5:1
- For every dollar on acquisition, make at least three back

### CAC Payback Period
- Median SaaS: 6.8 months (one source) to 18 months (Benchmarkit 2025 report, up from 14 months prior year)
- B2C apps: 4.2 months (lower CAC, faster activation)
- B2B SaaS: 8.6 months (acceptable given higher LTV)
- Ideal: less than 12 months
- Private SaaS average: 23 months
- Less than 12 months is ideal for SaaS

### Activation Rate
- One of the most important experience KPIs alongside retention rate and time-to-value
- PQLs convert at 25-30% vs MQLs at 5-10%

### Retention
- Non-consumer-social apps: retention should be above 50%
- B2B SaaS average monthly churn: 5.2%
- Target NRR for PLG: 120%+

### Freemium Conversion
- Average: 5% of freemium signups convert to paid (OpenView benchmark)

### How Metrics Differ by Stage

**Early stage (pre-PMF):**
- Focus: activation rate, retention (is the product sticky?), qualitative feedback
- CAC tolerance: high (learning, not optimizing)
- Key question: do users come back?

**Growth stage ($1M-$20M ARR):**
- Focus: CAC payback, channel efficiency, conversion rates at each funnel stage
- LTV:CAC must be trending toward 3:1
- Start building attribution infrastructure

**Scale stage ($20M+ ARR):**
- Focus: NRR, expansion revenue, blended CAC across channels, marginal CAC
- Diminishing returns on existing channels; need new channels
- Sophisticated attribution and incrementality testing

## 4. Current Trends and Shifts

### A. AI-Generated Content at Scale

- AI referral traffic up 357% YoY (June 2025), but still only 1.08% of total traffic
- Pure AI content performs poorly long-term: only 3% of pages remain in top 100 after 3 months
- Successful approach: AI-assisted content production with human editing, expertise, and E-E-A-T signals
- In health-related queries, AI Overviews shown in 82.5% of searches; in e-commerce, only 2%

### B. Third-Party Cookies and Attribution

**Current status (as of late 2025):**
- Google paused full deprecation in Chrome; instead, new privacy controls let users manage data sharing
- Third-party cookies remain enabled by default in Chrome (subject to technical requirements)
- Safari and Firefox already block third-party cookies

**Attribution challenges:**
- Cross-site tracking gaps: browsers block tracking pixels, can't connect sessions to original ad clicks
- Retargeting pools shrunk dramatically: smaller, less accurate, refresh more slowly
- Campaign performance data less detailed; harder to measure success and optimize spend

**Adaptation strategies:**
- First-party data is king: 71% of publishers recognize it as key (up from 64% in 2024); 85% expect its role to increase in 2026
- Server-side tracking and clean rooms
- Google Analytics 4 (GA4) for cookieless measurement
- Consent-based, trust-built strategies

### C. Dark Social and Word-of-Mouth Measurement

- Elena Verna emphasizes prioritizing owned and earned channels (virality, word of mouth, UGC) over renting access to others' audiences via paid channels
- Dark social (private shares via DMs, Slack, text) is notoriously hard to measure but increasingly recognized as a major growth driver
- Influencer marketing and social media experts predict a resurgence of long-form content with sharper focus on measuring influencer campaign performance

### D. Influencer Marketing

- Influencer marketing continues to grow as a channel
- Creator commerce and YouTube partnerships trending up
- B2B influencer marketing gaining traction (not just B2C)
- Key challenge: measurement and attribution of influencer-driven conversions

### E. Rise of Reddit as a Marketing Channel

- Reddit is increasingly seen as a growth marketing channel in 2025
- Authentic community participation (not overt advertising) is the effective approach
- Reddit ads gaining traction as a paid channel alongside Meta and Google

### F. Pricing Upheaval (Kyle Poyar / Growth Unhinged data)

- Among top 500 SaaS/AI companies, 1,800+ pricing changes in 2025 (3.6 per company)
- 2025 is the year everyone lost confidence in their pricing
- Seats and flat-rate pricing under threat as AI disconnects value from login counts
- Credit models exploded: up 126% YoY
- Hybrid pricing (subscription + usage) is the "it" model
- Outcome-based pricing is the "holy grail" but out of reach for 95% of the market
- Usage-based pricing gaining ground as AI reshapes value perception

## 5. Frameworks Growth Marketers Use

### AARRR Pirate Metrics (Dave McClure)
- Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue
- Useful as a mental model for the customer journey stages
- **Limitation (per Balfour)**: doesn't connect funnel output to input; misses compounding effect of growth strategies

### Growth Loops vs. Funnels (Brian Balfour / Reforge)
- Funnels: linear with defined start and end point
- Growth loops: output gets reinvested into the first step, creating compound interest effect
- "Growth loops compound momentum, whereas funnels run out of fuel" (Balfour)
- Types: viral loops, content loops, paid loops, sales loops
- The fastest growing products grow via loops, not funnels

### Brian Balfour's Four Fits Framework
Emerged from studying how companies like HubSpot and Canva achieved $100M+ growth:
1. **Product-Market Fit**: product solves a real problem
2. **Product-Channel Fit**: product is designed for how customers discover it
3. **Channel-Model Fit**: business model economics work with chosen channel
4. **Model-Market Fit**: how you sell/charge matches how your market wants to buy

All four fits must be in harmony. Channel selection is not arbitrary; it must fit the product, model, and market.

**Updated for AI era**: Balfour published "The Four Fits: A Growth Framework for the AI Era" extending the framework for AI-native companies.

### ICE Framework (Sean Ellis)
- **Impact**: how big a difference will this make to our key metric?
- **Confidence**: how sure are you it will work?
- **Ease**: how quickly and cheaply can you implement?
- Score: Impact x Confidence x Ease
- Created at LogMeIn and Dropbox; popular among growth teams for quick, data-informed prioritization

### PIE Framework (Widerfunnel)
- **Potential**: how much improvement can be made?
- **Importance**: how valuable is the traffic to this page?
- **Ease**: how difficult is the test?
- Similar to ICE but swaps Confidence for Potential

### North Star Metric
- Single metric the entire growth team rallies around
- Makes prioritization clearer, aligns decisions with real user value, experiments easier to judge
- Examples: Airbnb = nights booked, Spotify = time spent listening, Slack = messages sent
- All experiment impact measured against North Star

### Emily Kramer's MKT1 Frameworks
- **GACC Framework**: Goals, Audience, Creative, Channel for aligning team and company
- **Story Stack (Story-verse)**: clarifies brand/story components and how they fit together
- **The MKT1 Method**: practical system for strategy, planning, execution, team building

### Elena Verna's Anti-Patterns (10 Tactics That Don't Work)
1. Hiring a head of growth too early
2. Expecting growth team to fix a declining business
3. Copying competitors' growth tactics (context-dependent)
4. Over-relying on paid channels (renting someone else's audience)
5. Spending time on minor optimizations (button colors, third-party sign-ups)
6. Not updating the growth model (every channel/loop has a lifespan)
7. Relying on one-off emails for growth
8. Rebranding/redesigning website expecting performance lift
9. Over-reliance on experiments alone for growth
10. Not evolving when channels lose momentum

## 6. Growth Marketing vs. Growth Engineering

### Growth Marketing Owns:
- Customer acquisition through channels: paid ads, SEO, content marketing
- Brand positioning, messaging, storytelling
- Campaign creation and optimization
- Channel strategy and budget allocation
- Top-of-funnel metrics: impressions, clicks, signups, MQLs
- Creative and messaging iteration

### Growth Engineering Owns:
- The entire customer journey optimization (acquisition through retention and referral)
- A/B testing infrastructure and experimentation platforms
- Onboarding flow optimization (code-level changes)
- Feature flagging and progressive rollouts
- Data pipelines and analytics infrastructure
- In-product growth mechanics (nudges, tooltips, upgrade prompts)
- Performance optimization affecting conversion
- Internal tools that remove engineering as a bottleneck for marketing iteration

### The Overlap:
- Growth marketing includes all the characteristics of growth engineering, but growth engineering only overlaps with a portion of growth marketing functions
- Growth engineering unifies engineering, product, and marketing into a single cross-functional team
- "One of the best ways an engineer can move a target metric is by removing themselves as a bottleneck, so colleagues from marketing can iterate and optimize freely"

### Key Differences in the AI Era:
- Growth engineers use AI to: automate personalization at scale, analyze qualitative feedback from thousands of users in seconds, auto-generate experiment hypotheses based on usage patterns, predict churn risk
- Growth marketers use AI to: generate ad creative, optimize bid strategies, personalize email content, create content at scale
- "Growth engineers are the ones building systems that learn. And in a competitive market, systems that learn are the ones that win."
- The strongest teams blur the lines between them

### Handoff Points:
- Marketing generates demand; engineering captures and activates it
- Marketing defines the audience; engineering builds the personalization systems
- Marketing runs the campaigns; engineering instruments the measurement
- Marketing identifies what messaging works; engineering scales it through the product
